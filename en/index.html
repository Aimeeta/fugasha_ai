ABOUT FUGASHA

I used to spend my days on getting through, not on the work I cared about.

What changed things was AI — a report that used to take hours was done in thirty minutes. I looked up from my desk and noticed it was still light outside. I couldn’t forget that feeling, so I built Fugasha around it.

I studied at Keio University in Tokyo and the University of Cologne, then spent years across design, PR, and marketing, alongside three years writing about cybersecurity. What that work taught me: a tool left on the shelf changes nothing. The job is shaping it to fit the way a business actually runs.

So Fugasha never starts with tools. It starts with one question — where would a change earn the most?

// PHILOSOPHY

Efficiency is a doorway, not the destination.

Three ideas at the root of how Fugasha works.

01

Subtract before you add.

Before bringing in a new tool, we look for work that can stop. What you remove draws the outline of what matters.

02

Absorption is a resource.

A person fully absorbed in their work outperforms any automation. The job of a system is to protect that state.

03

Room makes decisions.

A head filled with tasks and appointments can’t think ahead. Blank space isn’t idleness — it’s where the next move goes.
